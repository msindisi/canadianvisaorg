Ontario INP

Ontario INP

  1. Home
  2. Canada Immigration Programs
  3. Provincial Nominee Program
  4. Ontario INP
Updated: May 23rd, 2022

Ontario is the most populous province in Canada, approximately 40 percent of Canadians live there. The province is home to Ottawa, Canada's capital city, as well as Toronto, the country's largest city and primary business and financial center. Ontario is the economic, social, and political heartland of Canada. It is by far the most popular destination in Canada for new immigrants from around the world, making it the most multicultural province in the country. The province includes more than 125 spoken languages and people from over 200 different countries. The easiest way to immigrate to Ontario, Canada is with a provincial nomination under the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP).    

What Is the Ontario Provincial Nominee Program (OINP)


The Ontario INP is designed to give select foreign nationals, interested in immigrating to Ontario, Canada the opportunity to apply for Canadian permanent residence through an expedited process. Formerly called the Opportunities Ontario Provincial Nominee Program (OOPNP), the program is designed for prospective immigrants who possess the specific skills and experience needed in the province. There are two different ways to go about getting nominated by the OINP. Outside of Express Entry, a potential immigrant can contact Immigration Ontario and apply to the OINP directly. If given a provincial nomination, he or she can then create a Canada Express Entry profile and add their nomination credentials. 

Prospective immigrants can also begin the process by creating an Express Entry profile and registering their interest in the province within the system. The OINP can then contact the person through Express Entry and formally invite them to apply to the immigration program. This will instantaneously increase their score under the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS)by 600 points. The person will likely then have enough Canada Express Entry CRS points to receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) during the next Express Entry draw, which takes place every few weeks.    

Below are the 3 main ways to immigrate to Canada under the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program: 


Ontario INP Categories
Employer Job Category
Human Capital Category
Business Category

Those who successfully apply to immigrate to Canada through the program can get permanent residence when they receive a Provincial Nomination (PN) from the province. In order to qualify for all PNPs you will also need the following:

  • Medical Certificate; 
  • Police Clearance; 
  • Biometrics; 
  • Security Checks; and 
  • Federal Checks.

If you succeed in getting a nomination from the province, you, your spouse or partner, and your dependent children can apply for permanent residence in Ontario. 

Employer Job Category


This category gives employers the ability to employ and keep skilled labourers and international students in the province. If you want to apply for any of the streams under this category, you must have a valid, full-time, permanent job offer from an employer in Ontario. 

The Employer Job Category has three subcategories:

  • International Student Stream; 
  • In-Demand Skills Stream; and 
  • Foreign Worker Stream;

International Student Stream

This stream is not currently accepting applicants.  

If you choose to apply for Canadian permanent residence through the International Student Stream, you must make sure that your offer of employment is for a full-time, permanent position in a skilled occupation listed on the National Occupation Classification (NOC) level 0, A, or B. The job must also meet Ontarios standard wage level for the occupation.

International students having a job offer in a specialized occupation are encouraged to apply. This stream allows foreign students both inside and outside of Canada who have a job offer in Ontario to apply. Following that, students will be nominated and granted permanent residency by Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). This is a terrific opportunity for students because Ontario is one of the most varied, lively, and thriving provinces in Canada. It's an excellent starting point for their future jobs. While the OINP has stringent requirements, successful candidates will have a fantastic opportunity to get permanent status through the Ontario PNP.

This stream is for foreign semi-skilled workers who are interested in immigrating to Ontario, Canada and have work experience and a job offer that is listed on the provinces in-demand jobs list.

This stream is not currently accepting applicants.

This stream is for highly skilled foreign workers who want to apply for Canadian permanent residence and have work experience and a job offer that is listed on the provinces in-demand jobs list.

New Regional Immigration Pilot

This two-year immigration pilot, which launched in July 2020, is aimed at helping smaller and rural communities in Ontario fill its labour gap. 150 nominations will now be reserved for skilled workers who want to immigrate to Canada under the Employer Job Offer category of the OINP. Applicants will need to have a valid full-time job offer in one of the participating communities as well as meet the general requirements of one of the three streams under this category.  

Participating communities include:

  • Chatham-Kent;
  • Cities of Belleville and Quinte West; and
  • The City of Cornwall.

To apply through the new regional pilot you will need to submit an intake form by email to the regional immigration offices, and if successful, also submit your application through the OINP.   

Human Capital Category


Under this category, you will find the most streams, five to be exact, including three Express Entry options to Ontario. Through this program, employers are able to quickly meet the human resources they need to grow and maintain their business. 

The Human Capital Category has three subcategories:   

  • Express Entry Human Capital Priorities Stream; 
  • Express Entry French-Speaking Skilled Worker Stream; 
  • Express Entry Skilled Trades Stream;
  • Masters Graduates Stream; and 
  • Ph.D. Graduates Stream.

Express Entry Human Capital Priorities Stream

Your eligibility for this stream is dependent on both federal and provincial criteria. Both of these sets of requirements need to be satisfied if you would like to apply for Canadian permanent residence under this stream. In terms of the federal requirements, you must: Have a valid profile in the Express Entry pool; Be eligible for the Canadian Experience Class (CEC) or Federal Skilled Worker Program; Indicate that you have an interest in immigrating to Ontario when you create your Express Entry profile; and Have received a Notification of Interest from Ontario.      


Express Entry Human Capital Priorities Category Eligibility Requirements
Express Entry ProfileYes
Connection to ProvinceNo
Job OfferNo
Language ProficiencyYes - at least CLB7 in either French or English
Level of EducationYes (a bachelor's degree or higher)
Work ExperienceYes. (at least one year of continuous work experience within the previous five years in NOC Skill Level 0, A or B); or
at least one year of cumulative work experience within the previous three years that is in line with the requirements of the Canadian Experience Class
AgeExpress Entry Requirements

OINP Tech Draws

Ontario is searching Express Entry draw pools for targeted indemand occupations. Candidates will have to meet the HUman Capital Priorities Stream as well as have work experience in one of the targeted occupations listed below.

Eligible OINP Occupations
  • Software engineers and desugners (NOC 2173)
  • Computer programmers and interacteive media developers (NOC 2174)
  • Compuyter engineers (NOC 2147)
  • Web designers and developers (NOC 2175)
  • Database analysts and data administrators
  • Computer and information systems managers (NOC0213)

Express Entry French-Speaking Skilled Worker Stream

The Ontario INP French-Speaking Skilled Worker stream is aligned with Canada's Express Entry selection system and enables prospective immigrants that speak French to move to Ontario, Canada. Your eligibility for this stream is dependent on both federal and provincial criteria. Both of these sets of requirements need to be satisfied if you would like to apply under this stream. In terms of the federal requirements, you must: Have a valid profile in the Express Entry pool; Be eligible for the Canadian Experience Class (CEC) or Federal Skilled Worker Program; Indicate that you have an interest in immigrating to Ontario when you create your Express Entry profile; and Have received a Notification of Interest from Ontario You may be eligible for the OINP French-Speaking Skilled Worker Stream if you satisfy all the criteria below.

Canadian Experience Class (CEC) - a minimum of one year, full-time employment in a NOC 0, A, or B level occupation in the past 36 months.

In terms of the federal requirements, you must: Have a valid profile in the Express Entry pool; Be eligible for the Canadian Experience Class (CEC) or Federal Skilled Worker Program; Indicate that you have an interest in immigrating to Ontario when you create your Express Entry profile; and Have received a Notification of Interest from Ontario.

Masters Graduate Stream

This stream is not currently accepting applicants. If you obtained a Master's Degree at a university in Ontario and would like to live and work in the province this is the stream for you. You dont even need a job offer in Ontario, Canada in order to qualify for the Master's Graduates Stream, however, you must have submitted your application no later than two years from the date of completion of your Master's degree. It can be submitted during your last semester of study in the program.

FAQs

Why should I get a OINP?

The Ontario PNP is an excellent program for anybody considering relocating to the Canadian province. Of course, it does not apply to everyone, but those who do would gain enormously from a nomination. To get the first Ontario nomination, you must be chosen through an OINP EOI draw after submitting an Expression of Interest to the province. A province nomination will add 600 points to your Canada Express Entry Comprehensive Ranking Score, generally known as the Express Entry CRS. This increases your chances of getting an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residency through the federal immigration procedure significantly.

How do you prove intention to reside in Ontario for OINP?

There are various ways you can show intent to reside in Ontario. Provide documentation such as letters from employers, a work contact if you have one, plans to rent or buy property, the addresses and contact of family or friends in Ontario, and any type of research youve been doing.

Do I need proof of funds for OINP?

This entirely depends on which program you apply for. For example, for the Human Capital Priorities (HCP) stream, you need to show minimum settlement funds unless youre applying for permanent residency through the Canadian Experience Class. Make sure you look at the requirements for the program you want to apply for.

Whats the best place to live in Ontario?

Ontario is home to the most famous city in Canada, Toronto. The city has thousands of jobs, offers a vivid lifestyle, plenty of entertainment, hospitals, schools and your choice of universities and colleges. Theres no denying that many people immigrating to Canada want to live in Toronto. However, if things dont work out that way, dont worry. Ontario has other great places to offer such as Ottowa and Hamilton. If you want a more quiet lifestyle, consider moving to Stratford or Collingwood. These are smaller towns in the province that offer a suburban feel for you and your family.

Immigration FAQs

1. What Is The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)?

Skilled foreign workers who want to immigrate to Ontario, can apply through the OINP, for a faster immigration process. The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program is for select immigrants, and their family members, who have the specific skills and experience needed by the province.

In addition, the idea behind OINP is that it is designed to enhance economic development in areas such as job creation and job retention for foreign immigrants.

The OINP is largely employer driven, which means that any job that a skilled foreign worker wishes to apply for, must be preapproved by the IRCC, (Immigration, Refugees Citizenship Canada) and all employers must be pre-screened.

2. How Do You Apply For The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)?

There are two ways you can go about applying for OINP (Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program):

  • You can directly contact and apply to the Province of Ontario:
    • If you get a Provincial Nomination, you must create a Canada Express Entry Profile and include your Ontario Nomination Certificate.
  • You can begin your process by creating a Canada Express Entry Profile:
    • You can register your interest in the Province of Ontario on your profile. The OINP can then contact you through Express Entry and invite you to apply (ITA) for the immigration program. If you are nominated by Ontario, your CRS will instantly increase by 600 points.

Candidates with enough CRS points will be Invited to Apply (ITA) for the next Express Entry draw.

3. What Are The Minimum Requirements To Immigrate To Ontario As A Skilled Worker Under The General Provincial Nominee Program?

If you want to immigrate to Ontario as a skilled worker for the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) you must:

  • Have a minimum of two years paid work experience within the past five years. The work experience you apply with, must match the job position you apply for in your application. If you have Canadian work experience, you must be able to prove and validate it;
  • Have a job offer from an Ontario employer, for a permanent, full-time position. The job must fall under the following National Occupation Classification (NOC) categories for skilled workers:
    • O: Management positions
    • A: Professional jobs
    • B: Technical jobs
  • There are two forms required for the application process the:
    • Joint Verification Form, signed by your employer; and the
    • Employer Pre-Screen Form
  • You must have the proper legal status to be in Canada if you are living in the country during your application process.

Your employer must also meet the following requirements:

  • The employers place of business must be situated in Ontario;
  • The employers business must be in operation for a minimum of three years;
  • The company must have:
    • A gross revenue of approximately $1,000,000, with at least five full time employees; or
    • A gross revenue of approximately $500,000, with a minimum of three full time employees
  • The employer must meet current wage stipulations for the position; and
  • The employer must also prove that the job is necessary for the operation of their company. The company must prove that they have not overlooked Canadian citizens for employment or training opportunities.

4. How Can International Students Apply For Permanent Residence In Ontario?

The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) has three specialized immigration categories for international students. Graduates from approved tertiary institutions can apply for permanent residency status if they meet the correct requirements for each category. The categories are the:

  • Pilot International Master's Graduate Category (applicant does not need a job offer);
  • International PhD Students Category (applicant does not need a job offer); and the
  • Job Offer Stream for international students (job offer is required)

5. What Are The Minimum Requirements To Apply For OINP International Student Programs?

Each of the three immigration streams under the OINP have different requirements to meet if you are an international student.

Pilot International Masters Graduate Category

If you wish to qualify for the Pilot International Masters Graduate Category, you must show that:

  • You intend to live and work in Ontario;
  • You did not reside in Ontario for at least one full year, during the past two years;
  • You are not an asylum seeker or refugee claimant;
  • You are proficient in English or French;
  • You are a graduate from an existing Master's program at an eligible Ontario University;
  • You got your Masters degree less than two years ago;
  • You have enough savings or income to support yourself and any other dependants; and
  • You plan to further your studies on a full-time basis

International PhD Students Category

If you want to qualify for the International PhD students Category, you must show that:

  • You went to an approved publicly funded University in Ontario;
  • You successfully graduated or completed at least two years of intended PhD studies;
  • You graduated with your PhD less than two years ago; and
  • You have the appropriate legal status in Canada.

International Student with a job offer Category

You can apply for the International Student with a job offer if:

  • You completed half of your studies in Canada;
  • You completed at least a two-year degree, diploma or certificate program;
  • You have a full-time job offer for a skilled occupation in Ontario and the job offer is approved by Opportunities Ontario;
  • You graduated with a degree, diploma or certificate, less than two years ago;
  • You have the appropriate legal status in Canada; and
  • You have a Joint Verification Form signed by your employer, and an Employer Pre-Screen Form.

6. What Is The Ontario Corporate Program And How Is It Related To The OINP?

The Ontario Corporate Program is for implementing, assisting and supporting international corporations that are looking to expand in Ontario, by means of purchasing or opening a business in the province. If the corporation is successfully established, some employees of the international corporation can apply for permanent residence under the OINP (Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program).

The OINP establishes a performance agreement with the potential corporation. The agreement includes performance goals and estimated time-frames for the Ontario corporation. If these conditions cannot be met, nominations for key staff members will not be processed.

7. What Are The Minimum Requirements For The Ontario Corporate Program?

The minimum conditions for a successful Corporate Program in Ontario are:

  • A maximum of five key foreign employees may be nominated for each successful Corporate Program;
  • A language test in English (that has the required scores) given to all key foreign nationals and submitted results within 24 months;
  • An investment of $5,000,000 or more should be rendered in the new or existing corporation;
  • An existing international business should have a well-established track record for a minimum of three years; and
  • The creation of five permanent job positions for Canadian citizens or Canadian permanent residents.

8. How Can French Speaking Immigrants Apply For Permanent Residence Under OINP?

A French speaking immigration stream is available under the Ontario Immigration Nominee Program (OINP). The French-Speaking Skilled Worker Stream enables French speaking foreign nationals to create an Express Entry Profile, whereby they can indicate their interest in Ontario.

French candidates in the Express Entry Pool may be eligible for the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP) or Canada Experience Class (CEC).

French speaking immigrants must meet the following requirements for the OINP:

  • French speaking candidates must have a tertiary qualification; Bachelor's degree, Masters Degree or PhD. If candidates did not receive their education from Canadian institutions, it must be confirmed that their education is on par with Canada's standards;
  • French candidates must obtain a high score in the Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) in French and English in order to be a successful applicant;
  • Potential applicants should have proof that they have enough funds to settle in the province;
  • French applicants for the Canadian Experience Class (CEC) must have Canadian work experience for at least one year as full-time skilled workers; and
  • French applicants applying under the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP), must have at least one year paid working experience as skilled workers

Take Note: French-speaking candidates who receive a nomination from an Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP), have 30 days to accept the nomination. This nomination will give candidates 600 additional points in the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS).

9. What Is The Ontario Human Capital Priorities Program (OHCPP)?

The Ontario Human Capital Priorities is a stream of the OINP (Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program) whereby skilled workers, with a CRS (Comprehensive Ranking System) score of 400 or more, can be chosen to live and work permanently in Ontario.

It is important for potential candidates to create an Express Entry Profile and indicate their interest in Ontario. To be considered for the Human Capital Priorities Stream candidates must meet all requirements, these include:

  • Obtaining a minimum of seven in the Canadian Language Benchmark (CLB) in English or French;
  • Graduating with a Bachelors Degree or higher;
  • Having one year of full-time work experience, gained in a skilled job position; and
  • Loving everything about Ontario

If candidates meet all the above requirements, they can be nominated for immigration under the Ontario Human Capital Priorities Program (OHCPP).

10. What Is The Ontario Entrepreneur Stream?

Foreign entrepreneurs who would like to create a business in Ontario or purchase an existing company can do so by means of the Ontario Entrepreneur Stream.

If your business plan is approved, you and your business partner (if applicable) may apply for a temporary business visa in order to establish the business. Once it has been established that the business activity will be economically beneficial to Ontario, you and your business partner may proceed to apply for permanent residence under the OINP (Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program).

11. How Do You Qualify For The Ontario Entrepreneur Stream?

In order for a potential entrepreneur or business partner to be eligible for the Ontario Entrepreneur Stream (OES), these five factors must be met:

  • Net Worth
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish the company within the Greater Toronto Area, he or she must have a minimum net worth of $1,500,000;
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish the company outside the Greater Toronto Area, he or she must have a minimum net worth of $800,000; or
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish a company in the line of Digital Communication or Information Communication (IC), he or she must have a minimum net worth of $800,000
  • Experience
    • The entrepreneur must have a minimum of 36 months worth of business experience; and
    • The entrepreneur must have experience as a business owner or work experience in a senior position
  • Job Creation
    • The entrepreneur is expected to employ at least two Canadian citizens or permanent residents on a full-time basis
  • Preliminary Visit
    • The entrepreneur is expected to have made a business-related visit to Canada, twelve months prior to submitting the application.
  • Personal Investment Proceeds
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish the company within the Greater Toronto Area, he or she must make a minimum investment of $1,000,000 and have a minimum share of 33.3%;
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish the company outside the Greater Toronto Area, he or she must make a minimum investment of $500,000 and have a minimum share of 33.3%; or
    • If the entrepreneur wants to establish a company in the line of Digital Communication or IC, he or she must make a minimum investment of $500,000 and have a minimum share of 33.3%.
